Life in Southwest Wisconsin An exploration of Southwest Wisconsin

Southwest Wisconsin, often referred to as the Driftless Area, is known and loved by the people who live there. However, not everyone may know about the treasure that is the Driftless Area. While you won't find any big cities with expansive skylines, southwest Wisconsin has much more to offer.

From a planning perspective, the Southwestern Wisconsin Regional Planning Commission (SWWRPC) oversees the area. SWWRPC is an extension of local government and provides low-cost, expert planning and economic development services to county, city, village, and town goverments. SWWRPC collaborates with communities and organizations to build capacity, advocate for its residents, and develop dynamic solutions for the challenges within the region. The solutions will strive for the best possible tomorrow while respecting the traditions that built the region. SWWRPC is one of nine regional planning commissions in Wisconsin and was created by an executive order in 1970. Check out SWWRPC's website for more information.

Moving forward, this website will explore the characteristics of southwest Wisconsin for both residents and nonresidents. While analyses may have a lens toward SWWRPC and the planning field, the maps within this website may be useful to viewers in many other fields as well.
